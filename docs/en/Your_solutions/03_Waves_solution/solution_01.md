<h2>1. Wave Properties</h2>

<p><strong>Question:</strong></p>
<p>
A sound wave in air has a frequency of 440 Hz. If the speed of sound in air is 343 m/s,
what is its wavelength? What is its wavelength in water, where the speed of sound is 1482 m/s?
</p>

<p><strong>Given:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Frequency: f = 440 Hz</li>
  <li>Speed of sound in air: v<sub>air</sub> = 343 m/s</li>
  <li>Speed of sound in water: v<sub>water</sub> = 1482 m/s</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Important idea:</strong></p>
<p>
The basic wave relation is:
</p>

<p>&lambda; = v / f</p>

<p>
This means the wavelength of a wave is equal to the wave speed divided by the frequency.
</p>

<p>
Here, the frequency stays the same when the wave moves from one medium to another.
Only the speed and the wavelength change.
</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Step 1: Find the wavelength in air</strong></p>

<p>Use the formula:</p>
<p>&lambda; = v / f</p>

<p>Substitute the values for air:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>air</sub> = 343 / 440</p>

<p>Calculate:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>air</sub> = 0.7795 m</p>

<p>Round to two decimal places:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>air</sub> &asymp; 0.78 m</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Step 2: Find the wavelength in water</strong></p>

<p>Use the same formula again:</p>
<p>&lambda; = v / f</p>

<p>Substitute the values for water:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>water</sub> = 1482 / 440</p>

<p>Calculate:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>water</sub> = 3.368 m</p>

<p>Round to two decimal places:</p>
<p>&lambda;<sub>water</sub> &asymp; 3.37 m</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Step 3: Compare the two results</strong></p>

<p>
The wavelength in water is much larger than the wavelength in air.
This happens because sound travels faster in water than in air.
</p>

<p>
Since frequency does not change, a larger speed means a larger wavelength.
</p>

<hr>

<p><strong>Final Answer:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Wavelength in air = 0.78 m</li>
  <li>Wavelength in water = 3.37 m</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Short explanation:</strong></p>
<p>
The formula &lambda; = v / f shows that wavelength depends directly on speed.
Because the speed of sound is much greater in water, the wavelength is also greater in water.
</p>
