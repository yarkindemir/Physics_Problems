<h2>5. Echo Ranging</h2>

<p><strong>Given:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Echo time: t = 1 s</li>
  <li>Speed of sound in air: v = 343 m/s</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Important idea:</strong></p>
<p>
When a person shouts toward a cliff, the sound first travels <strong>from the person to the cliff</strong>,
then the reflected sound travels <strong>from the cliff back to the person</strong>.
So the measured time of 1 second is the time for the <strong>round trip</strong>, not just one way.
</p>

<p><strong>Step 1: Write the distance formula</strong></p>
<p>distance = speed × time</p>

<p>So for the total sound path:</p>
<p>total distance = v × t</p>

<p>total distance = 343 × 1 = 343 m</p>

<p><strong>Step 2: Relate total distance to the distance from the cliff</strong></p>
<p>
If the cliff is d meters away, then:
</p>

<p>total distance = 2d</p>

<p>So:</p>
<p>2d = 343</p>

<p><strong>Step 3: Solve for d</strong></p>
<p>d = 343 / 2</p>

<p>d = 171.5 m</p>

<p><strong>Final Answer:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>The cliff is 171.5 m away.</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Short explanation:</strong></p>
<p>
We divide by 2 because the sound traveled to the cliff and came back.
The 1 second includes both parts of the trip.
</p>
