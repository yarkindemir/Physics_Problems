<h2>6. Wave Equation</h2>

<p><strong>Given wave equation:</strong></p>
<p>y(x,t) = 0.05 sin(2&pi;x - 50&pi;t)</p>

<p><strong>Compare with the standard form of a traveling wave:</strong></p>
<p>y(x,t) = A sin(kx - &omega;t)</p>

<p>By comparing the two equations, we identify:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Amplitude: A = 0.05 m</li>
  <li>Wave number: k = 2&pi; rad/m</li>
  <li>Angular frequency: &omega; = 50&pi; rad/s</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>a) Amplitude</strong></p>
<p>
The amplitude is the maximum displacement from equilibrium.
It is the number in front of the sine function.
</p>

<p>A = 0.05 m</p>

<p><strong>b) Wavelength</strong></p>
<p>Use the formula:</p>
<p>k = 2&pi; / &lambda;</p>

<p>Substitute k = 2&pi;:</p>
<p>2&pi; = 2&pi; / &lambda;</p>

<p>Divide both sides by 2&pi;:</p>
<p>1 = 1 / &lambda;</p>

<p>So:</p>
<p>&lambda; = 1 m</p>

<p><strong>c) Frequency</strong></p>
<p>Use the formula:</p>
<p>&omega; = 2&pi;f</p>

<p>Substitute &omega; = 50&pi;:</p>
<p>50&pi; = 2&pi;f</p>

<p>Divide both sides by 2&pi;:</p>
<p>f = 25 Hz</p>

<p><strong>d) Wave speed</strong></p>
<p>Use the formula:</p>
<p>v = f&lambda;</p>

<p>Substitute the values:</p>
<p>v = 25 × 1 = 25 m/s</p>

<p><strong>You can also check with:</strong></p>
<p>v = &omega; / k</p>

<p>v = (50&pi;) / (2&pi;) = 25 m/s</p>

<p><strong>Final Answers:</strong></p>
<ul>
  <li>Amplitude: 0.05 m</li>
  <li>Wavelength: 1 m</li>
  <li>Frequency: 25 Hz</li>
  <li>Wave speed: 25 m/s</li>
</ul>
