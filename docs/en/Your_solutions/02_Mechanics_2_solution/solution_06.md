## 6. Energy Dissipation

A tennis ball is dropped from a height of $2.0\ \text{m}$.

After each bounce, it loses 30% of its mechanical energy.

Find the height after the second bounce.

---

### Step 1: Understand the energy loss

If the ball loses 30% of its energy, then it keeps 70%:

$$
70\% = 0.7
$$

The maximum height after each bounce is also multiplied by $0.7$.

---

### Step 2: Height after the first bounce

Initial height:

$$
h_0 = 2.0\ \text{m}
$$

After the first bounce:

$$
h_1 = 0.7 \times 2.0
$$

$$
h_1 = 1.4\ \text{m}
$$

---

### Step 3: Height after the second bounce

$$
h_2 = 0.7 \times 1.4
$$

$$
h_2 = 0.98\ \text{m}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{h_2 = 0.98\ \text{m}}
$$
