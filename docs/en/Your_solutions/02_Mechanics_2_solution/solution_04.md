## 4. Energy and Momentum

A 0.5 kg block slides down a frictionless track from a height of 3.0 m. At the bottom, it collides and sticks to a 1.5 kg block, which is initially at rest.

Find the speed of the combined mass just after the collision.

---

### Step 1: Find the speed of the first block before collision

Because the track is frictionless, potential energy becomes kinetic energy:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels out:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v = \sqrt{2(9.81)(3.0)}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{58.86}
$$

$$
v \approx 7.67\ \text{m/s}
$$

So, the 0.5 kg block reaches the bottom with speed:

$$
v_1 \approx 7.67\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

### Step 2: Use momentum conservation for the collision

The blocks stick together, so this is an inelastic collision.

Formula:

$$
m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1 + m_2)v_f
$$

Given:

- $m_1 = 0.5\ \text{kg}$
- $v_1 = 7.67\ \text{m/s}$
- $m_2 = 1.5\ \text{kg}$
- $v_2 = 0$

Substitute:

$$
0.5(7.67) + 1.5(0) = (0.5 + 1.5)v_f
$$

$$
3.835 = 2v_f
$$

$$
v_f = \frac{3.835}{2}
$$

$$
v_f \approx 1.92\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{v_f \approx 1.92\ \text{m/s}}
$$
