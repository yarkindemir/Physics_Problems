## 5. Inelastic Collision

A 70 kg runner moving at $3\ \text{m/s}$ jumps onto a 140 kg stationary cart.

Find the final speed of the cart with the runner.

Is kinetic energy conserved in this collision?

---

### Step 1: Use momentum conservation

Because the runner and cart move together after the collision, this is an **inelastic collision**.

Formula:

$$
m_1v_1 + m_2v_2 = (m_1+m_2)v_f
$$

Given:

- $m_1 = 70\ \text{kg}$
- $v_1 = 3\ \text{m/s}$
- $m_2 = 140\ \text{kg}$
- $v_2 = 0$

Substitute:

$$
70(3) + 140(0) = (70+140)v_f
$$

$$
210 = 210v_f
$$

$$
v_f = 1\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

### Step 2: Check kinetic energy

Initial kinetic energy:

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}m_1v_1^2
$$

$$
K_i = \frac{1}{2}(70)(3^2)
$$

$$
K_i = 35 \cdot 9
$$

$$
K_i = 315\ \text{J}
$$

Final kinetic energy:

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(m_1+m_2)v_f^2
$$

$$
K_f = \frac{1}{2}(210)(1^2)
$$

$$
K_f = 105\ \text{J}
$$

Since:

$$
315 \ne 105
$$

kinetic energy is **not conserved**.

---

## Final Answers

Final speed:

$$
\boxed{v_f = 1\ \text{m/s}}
$$

Kinetic energy:

$$
\boxed{\text{Kinetic energy is not conserved}}
$$

This is because the collision is inelastic.
