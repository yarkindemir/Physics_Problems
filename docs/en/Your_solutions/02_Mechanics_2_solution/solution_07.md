## 7. Dynamics with Friction

A 5 kg block is placed on a 10 kg block.  
A horizontal force of 45 N is applied to the 10 kg block.  
The 5 kg block is tied to the wall.  
The coefficient of kinetic friction is $\mu = 0.2$.

Find the acceleration of the 10 kg block.

---

### Step 1: Friction between the two blocks

Formula:

$$
f_1 = \mu m_1 g
$$

$$
f_1 = 0.2 \times 5 \times 9.81
$$

$$
f_1 = 9.81\ \text{N}
$$

---

### Step 2: Friction with the ground

Total mass on ground:

$$
m_{total} = 5 + 10 = 15\ \text{kg}
$$

$$
f_2 = \mu m_{total} g
$$

$$
f_2 = 0.2 \times 15 \times 9.81
$$

$$
f_2 = 29.43\ \text{N}
$$

---

### Step 3: Net force

$$
F_{net} = 45 - f_1 - f_2
$$

$$
F_{net} = 45 - 9.81 - 29.43
$$

$$
F_{net} = 5.76\ \text{N}
$$

---

### Step 4: Acceleration

Only the 10 kg block moves:

$$
a = \frac{F_{net}}{m}
$$

$$
a = \frac{5.76}{10}
$$

$$
a = 0.576\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{a = 0.576\ \text{m/s}^2}
$$
