## 2. Harmonic Motion

A 10 kg mass is attached to a spring and oscillates according to:

$$
x(t) = 0.2\cos(10\pi t)
$$

Find:

- Spring constant $k$
- Total mechanical energy $E$

---

### Step 1: Identify values

From the equation:

$$
x(t) = A\cos(\omega t)
$$

We get:

$$
A = 0.2\ \text{m}, \quad \omega = 10\pi
$$

Mass:

$$
m = 10\ \text{kg}
$$

---

### Step 2: Find spring constant $k$

Formula:

$$
\omega = \sqrt{\frac{k}{m}}
$$

Square both sides:

$$
\omega^2 = \frac{k}{m}
$$

Solve for $k$:

$$
k = m\omega^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
k = 10(10\pi)^2
$$

$$
k = 10 \times 100\pi^2
$$

$$
k = 1000\pi^2
$$

$$
k \approx 9869.6\ \text{N/m}
$$

---

### Step 3: Find total mechanical energy

Formula:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}kA^2
$$

Substitute:

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.2)^2
$$

$$
E = \frac{1}{2}(1000\pi^2)(0.04)
$$

$$
E = 20\pi^2
$$

$$
E \approx 197.4\ \text{J}
$$

---

## Final Answers

Spring constant:

$$
\boxed{k \approx 9869.6\ \text{N/m}}
$$

Total energy:

$$
\boxed{E \approx 197.4\ \text{J}}
$$
