## 3. Conservation of Energy

A pendulum with a length of 1.0 meter is released from an initial angle of $15^\circ$.

Find the speed of the pendulum bob at the bottom of its swing.

---

### Step 1: Use energy conservation

At the top, the pendulum has gravitational potential energy.

At the bottom, this energy becomes kinetic energy.

So:

$$
mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2
$$

Mass cancels out:

$$
gh = \frac{1}{2}v^2
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

---

### Step 2: Find the height difference

The height is:

$$
h = L(1-\cos\theta)
$$

Given:

- $L = 1.0\ \text{m}$
- $\theta = 15^\circ$

So:

$$
h = 1(1-\cos15^\circ)
$$

$$
h = 1 - 0.9659
$$

$$
h \approx 0.0341\ \text{m}
$$

---

### Step 3: Find the speed

Now use:

$$
v = \sqrt{2gh}
$$

Substitute:

$$
v = \sqrt{2(9.81)(0.0341)}
$$

$$
v = \sqrt{0.669}
$$

$$
v \approx 0.82\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

## Final Answer

$$
\boxed{v \approx 0.82\ \text{m/s}}
$$
