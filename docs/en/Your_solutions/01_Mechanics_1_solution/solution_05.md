## 5. Relative Velocity

A river flows east at $2 \text{m/s}$. A boat that can travel at $5 \text{m/s}$ in still water wants to go directly north across the river. In what direction (angle) should it head? How long will it take to cross the river if it's 200 meters wide?


### Given:
- River velocity = 2 m/s (east)
- Boat speed = 5 m/s
- Boat must go directly north

### Step 1: Horizontal component must cancel river

5 sin(θ) = 2

sin(θ) = 2 / 5

θ = sin⁻¹(2 / 5) ≈ 23.58°

Direction:
23.58° west of north

---

### Step 2: Northward velocity

v_north = 5 cos(θ)

cos(23.58°) ≈ 0.917

v_north ≈ 5 × 0.917 ≈ 4.58 m/s

---

### Step 3: Time to cross

t = 200 / 4.58 ≈ 43.6 s

---

### Final Answer:
- Direction: 23.58° west of north
- Time: 43.6 s
