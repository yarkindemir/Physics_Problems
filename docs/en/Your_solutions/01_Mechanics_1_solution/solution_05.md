## 5. Relative Velocity

A river flows east at $2\ \text{m/s}$. A boat that can travel at $5\ \text{m/s}$ in still water wants to go directly north across the river.

Find:

- the direction (angle) the boat should head
- the time to cross a river that is $200$ meters wide

---

### Step 1: Understand the situation

The river current pushes the boat to the east with speed:

$$
v_{river} = 2\ \text{m/s}
$$

The boat speed in still water is:

$$
v_{boat} = 5\ \text{m/s}
$$

The boat wants to move **directly north**, so its west-east motion must cancel out.

That means the boat must aim a little **west of north**.

---

### Step 2: Find the angle

Let $\theta$ be the angle **west of north**.

The horizontal component of the boat's velocity must cancel the river speed:

$$
5\sin\theta = 2
$$

Now solve for $\theta$:

$$
\sin\theta = \frac{2}{5}
$$

$$
\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{2}{5}\right)
$$

$$
\theta \approx 23.58^\circ
$$

So the boat must head:

$$
\boxed{23.6^\circ\ \text{west of north}}
$$

---

### Step 3: Find the northward speed

The northward component is:

$$
v_{north} = 5\cos\theta
$$

Substitute $\theta = 23.58^\circ$:

$$
v_{north} = 5\cos(23.58^\circ)
$$

$$
v_{north} \approx 5(0.917)
$$

$$
v_{north} \approx 4.58\ \text{m/s}
$$

---

### Step 4: Find the crossing time

Use:

$$
t = \frac{d}{v}
$$

The river width is:

$$
d = 200\ \text{m}
$$

So:

$$
t = \frac{200}{4.58}
$$

$$
t \approx 43.7\ \text{s}
$$

---

## Final Answers

Direction:

$$
\boxed{23.6^\circ\ \text{west of north}}
$$

Time to cross:

$$
\boxed{43.7\ \text{s}}
$$
